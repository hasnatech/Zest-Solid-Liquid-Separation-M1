Working Principle of Decanter Centrifuge
  • The decanter centrifuge uses centrifugal forces that can be well beyond 3000 times greater than gravity
  • Solid particles are pressed outwards against the rotating bowl wall, while the less dense liquid phase forms a concentric inner layer
  • The sediment formed by the solids is continuously removed by the screw conveyor, which rotates at a different speed than the bowl
  • The solids are gradually “ploughed” out and removed from the conical part as a result

Decanter Parameters
Decanter Parameters

Differential Speed

Differential speed (Δn) → Difference between the bowl and screw speed.

If the differential speed is too high, it leads to:

  • Clean filtrate
  • Wet cake
  • Under utilization of decanter volume
  • Low torque of the screw

If the differential speed is too low, it leads to:

  • Dirty filtrate
  • Dry cake
  • High torque of the screw

Pond Depth

Pond depth (impeller) → Controls the liquid level inside the decanter.

If the depth of the pond is deep, it leads to:

  • More room for liquid clarification
  • Clean filtrate
  • Wet cake

If the pond is shallow, it leads to:

  • Dirty filtrate
  • Dry cake
